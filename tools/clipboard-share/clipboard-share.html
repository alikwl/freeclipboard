    Share Clipboard Online — No Download, No Signup

    FreeClipboard's Share Clipboard tool lets you instantly turn any text into a shareable link and QR code. Whether you're sending a code snippet from your laptop to your phone, sharing meeting notes with a colleague, or transferring a password between devices, this tool makes it effortless — no apps to install, no accounts to create.

    Everything works through URL encoding. Your text is converted to Base64 and embedded directly in the link's hash fragment. That means the link itself is the content — no data is ever stored on our servers. Open the link on any device, any browser, and the text appears instantly. It's the fastest way to share clipboard content between devices.

    How to Use the Clipboard Share Tool

    1. Paste Your Text: Type or paste up to 5,000 characters of text into the input area on the left.
    2. Generate the Link: Click "Generate Shareable Link" to create a unique URL containing your text and a scannable QR code.
    3. Share It: Copy the link, scan the QR code, or share directly via WhatsApp, Telegram, or email.
    4. Open Anywhere: Anyone who opens the link will instantly see your shared text — on any device, any browser, no login required.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is my shared text private?
    Your text is encoded in the URL using Base64 — it's not encrypted. Anyone who has the link can decode and read the text. Don't share sensitive passwords or confidential information through this tool. For sensitive data, use end-to-end encrypted messaging apps.
    Do shared links expire?
    No. Since the text is embedded directly in the URL (not stored on a server), the link works as long as this page exists. The expiry selector in the UI is cosmetic — links are truly permanent and self-contained.
    Is there a character limit?
    Yes, we limit text to 5,000 characters to keep URLs manageable. Very long URLs can cause issues with some browsers, messaging apps, and QR code scanners. For larger text, consider using our Online Notepad tool instead.
    How does the QR code work?
    The QR code encodes the full shareable URL. Scan it with any phone camera or QR scanner app, and it will open the link in your browser — instantly showing the shared text. It's the fastest way to transfer text from a computer to a phone.
    Can I edit text after sharing?
    Each link is a snapshot of the text at the time you generated it. To share updated text, simply modify it and generate a new link. The old link will still show the original text.
