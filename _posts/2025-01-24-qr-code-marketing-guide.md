## QR Code Design Best Practices

### 1. Size and Placement

**Minimum Size Guidelines:**
- **Print**: 2cm x 2cm (0.8" x 0.8")
- **Billboards**: Scale up proportionally
- **Business cards**: 1.5cm x 1.5cm minimum
- **Packaging**: 3cm x 3cm recommended

**Scanning Distance Formula:**
QR Code Size = Scanning Distance √∑ 10

**Example:** For 1-meter scanning distance, use 10cm QR code.

### 2. Color and Contrast

**Essential Rules:**
- **High contrast** is critical (dark on light)
- **Foreground must be darker** than background
- **Avoid light colors** (yellow, light blue, pink)
- **Test before printing** on actual materials

**Safe Color Combinations:**
‚úÖ Black on white (best)
‚úÖ Dark blue on white
‚úÖ Dark green on light gray
‚úÖ Purple on cream

**Avoid:**
‚ùå Yellow on white
‚ùå Light gray on white
‚ùå Red on black
‚ùå Low contrast combinations

### 3. Error Correction Levels

**QR Code Error Correction:**
- **L (Low)**: 7% recovery - Use for digital displays
- **M (Medium)**: 15% recovery - Standard use
- **Q (Quartile)**: 25% recovery - Recommended for print
- **H (High)**: 30% recovery - Use when adding logos

**Recommendation:** Use Q or H level for marketing materials.

### 4. Adding Logos and Branding

**Logo Guidelines:**
- Maximum 20-30% of QR code area
- Center placement
- Use high error correction (H level)
- Test thoroughly before deployment
- Maintain brand consistency

**Design Tips:**
- Keep logo simple
- Use high contrast
- Ensure scannability
- Test on multiple devices

## Common QR Code Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: No Clear Value Proposition

‚ùå **Problem:** Generic "Scan me" with no indication of benefit

‚úÖ **Solution:** Clear CTA explaining what users get
- "Scan for 20% off"
- "Scan to watch demo"
- "Scan for free guide"

### Mistake #2: Poor Placement

‚ùå **Problem:**
- Too high or too low
- Behind glass or plastic
- In hard-to-reach locations
- On moving objects

‚úÖ **Solution:**
- Eye level placement
- Easy access
- Stable surface
- Good lighting

### Mistake #3: Non-Mobile-Optimized Landing Pages

‚ùå **Problem:**
- Desktop-only design
- Slow loading
- Difficult navigation
- Requires zooming

‚úÖ **Solution:**
- Mobile-first design
- Fast loading (under 3 seconds)
- Large, tappable buttons
- Minimal scrolling

### Mistake #4: Too Small QR Codes

‚ùå **Problem:** QR code too small to scan easily

‚úÖ **Solution:** Follow size guidelines
- Minimum 2cm x 2cm for print
- Test from intended scanning distance
- Scale appropriately for medium

### Mistake #5: No Tracking

‚ùå **Problem:** Can't measure campaign success

‚úÖ **Solution:**
- Use trackable URLs
- Implement analytics
- Monitor key metrics
- A/B test variations

### Mistake #6: Ignoring Context

‚ùå **Problem:**
- QR codes where WiFi is unavailable
- Codes on moving vehicles
- Codes in areas with poor lighting

‚úÖ **Solution:**
- Consider user environment
- Ensure connectivity
- Provide adequate lighting
- Test in actual conditions

### Mistake #7: Overcomplicating

‚ùå **Problem:**
- Too much information in QR code
- Complex multi-step processes
- Confusing landing pages

‚úÖ **Solution:**
- Keep it simple
- Single, clear action
- Streamlined experience
- Minimal friction
